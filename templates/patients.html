Safety Protocols

ID Badges: All visitors must check-in at the Information Desk to receive a visitor pass.

Symptom Check: Please postpone your visit if you are experiencing fever or cold symptoms.

Hand Hygiene: Use the sanitizer stations located at every entrance and ward door.

Full Safety & Security Policy

1. Visitor Identification

All visitors must check in at the main information desk or any security kiosk. A valid government-issued photo ID is required to issue a temporary visitor pass. This pass must be worn visibly at all times while on the hospital premises.

2. Emergency Protocols

In the event of an emergency (Code Red, Blue, or Silver), follow the directions of the hospital staff immediately. Evacuation maps are posted near all elevator banks and stairwell entrances. Do not use elevators during fire alarms.

3. Photography & Privacy

To protect patient privacy and confidentiality (HIPAA compliance), photography or video recording of patients, staff, or medical equipment is strictly prohibited without explicit written consent from the hospital administration.

Zero Tolerance: City General Hospital maintains a zero-tolerance policy for aggressive behavior, harassment, or verbal abuse toward our medical staff and employees.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find a patient's room?

You can use our Interactive Navigator at any kiosk in the lobby or on your mobile device. Simply type the patient's name or department, and the AI will guide you with step-by-step instructions.

Are children allowed to visit?

Children under 12 are welcome but must be accompanied by an adult at all times. Some restricted areas like the ICU may have age limitations for safety reasons.

Where can I get a visitor pass?

Visitor passes are issued at the Main Information Desk in the lobby upon presentation of a valid photo ID.

Need More Assistance?

Our support team is available 24/7 to help you with any questions or special requirements you may have during your visit.
